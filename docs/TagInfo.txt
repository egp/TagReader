What your TagInfo fields now suggest

Taken together, your readout is consistent with a MIFARE Plus EV1, 2 KB variant, 7-byte UID, 17 pF antenna-capacitance version. The “2048 Bytes” is the key clue for the 2 KB size, and the “17 pF” field is best interpreted as the chip’s input/resonance capacitance variant, which matters for antenna/card design, not for how you parse the application data. NXP uses that same 17 pF / 70 pF distinction across MIFARE product variants.

Your UID 0x04241E12261290 is 7 bytes, which also fits a common MIFARE Plus packaging/configuration pattern. NXP’s product families and parametric listings include 2 KB and 4 KB variants with 7-byte UID options and 17 pF variants.

The most important clue is ISO14443-4

The line Technologies Supported - MIFARE, ISO14443-3, ISO14443-4 is the biggest practical clue for your Arduino plan.

That means you should not think of this only as a “MIFARE Classic-style sector reader” problem. NXP’s identification note says the MIFARE Plus family supports ISO/IEC 14443-4 (T=CL), and NXP’s MIFARE Plus application note explicitly says that MIFARE Plus in SL1/SL2 can still be activated up to ISO/IEC 14443-4 even when the SAK does not indicate it. In other words, your tag may look partly “Classic-like” at first, but still expect a layer-4 path for some operations.

That materially improves feasibility, because the PN532 can operate as a T=CL / ISO/IEC 14443-4 reader, not just as a simple MIFARE polling device. NXP’s PN532 application note states that in this mode the PN532 behaves as an ISO/IEC 14443-4 reader and can communicate with ISO/IEC 14443-4 cards.

What this means for your project

This pushes the likely architecture in a clearer direction:

More likely than before
You will be able to detect the tag reliably.
You will be able to confirm identity and low-level parameters.
You will probably want a library path that can send raw low-level exchanges, not only “read block N after Classic auth.”
Less likely to be enough
A stock Arduino “MIFARE Classic example sketch” by itself may not be sufficient.
If the useful payload is behind MIFARE Plus auth or AES-secured commands, you may need more than the usual hobby examples. NXP’s public MIFARE Plus documentation discusses SL1/SL3 and AES-authentication flows, which is a much more advanced path than ordinary Classic block reads.
